Earth and Mars

  • Earth and Mars share striking similarities as terrestrial planets with solid surfaces, four distinct seasons and the presence of frozen water.
  • Their near-identical axial tilts trigger similar long-term climate shifts, while both experience wind, clouds, and dust devils.
  • Both planets share familiar landscapes like giant shield volcanoes, deep canyons, and ancient river valleys.
  • Mars once possessed a strong, protective global magnetic field, much like the one that safeguards Earth today.
